Lady Macbeth

Lady Macbeth is the driving force behind Macbeth's actions during the play. It was Macbeth's idea, of course, to kill Duncan to become king, but when Macbeth faces doubt and guilt, Lady Macbeth pushes him to get on with the plan.

Throughout most of the play, Lady Macbeth is portrayed as clever and quick-witted. She saves Macbeth from accidentally confessing their actions when Macbeth sees Banquo's ghost at the dinner party, for example. However, by the end of the play, Lady Macbeth has succumbed to her own guilt and has gone crazy.

In Act 5, Lady Macbeth screams right before a servant tells Macbeth that she has died. It's implied that it was a suicide, but her death isn't shown onstage, so how exactly she died is up to interpretation. What is known, however, is that Macbeth had become so egocentric that he doesn't really care when told that his wife just died.
